Liturgical chant is a central part of Armenian culture and history. This rich tradition dates back to the founding of Armenian monasteries as early as the 4th century AD!

Patarag, by Makar Yekmalyan

  • “Patarag” means “Divine Liturgy” in Armenian and is the sacred mass of the Armenian Church. It has been practiced for centuries.
  • Yekmalyan was an Armenian composer who studied in the Armenian seminary of Echmiadzin and later in St. Petersburg under Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov.
  • In 1892, Yekmalyan composed a musical arrangement of the Patarag. His music follows a Western style of composition.

Patarag, by Komitas

  • Komitas was an Armenian priest and a successful composer and choirmaster.
  • In 1933, Komitas composed a different arrangement of the Patarag, taking inspiration from Yekmalyan and other composers.
  • Komitas’s arrangement introduces polyphony, secular music style, and incorporates more traditional Armenian folk music. It has a more secular, oriental feel.
